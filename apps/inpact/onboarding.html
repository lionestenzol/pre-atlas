in-PACT
Welcome. Let's set you up.
in-PACT is a planning system built from a real sales training binder. It connects what you do today to what you're trying to accomplish this month. This setup takes about 5 minutes.
Step 1 of 4: Your Why
Before you plan anything, you need to know why you're doing it. In the original system this was Step 7: Know Your Why. Your mission is the concrete thing you're working toward. Your motto is the sentence that snaps you back when the day falls apart. These live at the top of your daily plan as a reminder.
Mission
The concrete thing. Not a vibe. The car note. The degree. The launch date. What are you actually chasing?
Motto
When the day cracks and you want to quit, what do you say to yourself? One line. Yours.
A-Z Tasks
Step 2 of 4: Monthly Milestones
In the original binder, new hires got an A-Z sheet. Each letter was a concrete milestone to cross off over 4 weeks: "Z: Background check", "Y: Learn impulse curve", all the way up to "A: 10 reasons." Your version works the same way. Each letter is one focused objective for this month. Not a category. Not a project. A specific thing you can cross off. Start with 3-5 real ones. You can always add more.
This Week
Step 3 of 4: Weekly Focus
Monthly milestones don't happen by accident. Each week, you pick one A-Z letter as your primary target and decide what counts as a win. You also set focus across 6 life areas called PIGPEN: Production, Image, Growth, Personal, Errands, Network. This keeps you from pouring everything into one area while the rest starves. Monday you set the week. Friday you close it and see what moved.
Primary Letter Which A-Z task are you attacking this week?
Pick the one letter that gets the most daily attention this week.
Week Counts If
One sentence. If this is true by Friday, the week was a win. Be specific.
PIGPEN Focus
For each area, write what you're doing this week. Leave blank if you're skipping it. Not every area needs attention every week, but none should be ignored for long.
Today
Step 4 of 4: Your First Daily Plan
This is the daily ritual. In the original system it was called "Plan Your Attack / Attack Your Plan." Every morning you write one win target, three priorities, and name the lever that turns a minimum day into a maximum one. Your priorities should link to an A-Z task or a PIGPEN area. That's how daily work becomes monthly progress. You'll fill this out every morning on the Today page.
Win Target Step 5: Work the Territory
If only one thing happens today, what is it? The single result that makes today count.
Top 3 Priorities Steps 3 + 7: Be Prepared + Know Your Why
Three things you're doing today. Use the Link dropdowns to connect each one to the A-Z task or PIGPEN area it serves. This is how the system knows your daily work is moving the monthly needle.
The Lever
Min happens if you show up. Max happens if you pull a lever. What's the one thing that turns today from baseline to stretch? Name it before the day starts.
