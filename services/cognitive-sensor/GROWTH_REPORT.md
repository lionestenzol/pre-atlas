**"Everyone/No one":**
> "I want to commit again to putting myself first before everyone else..."
> "Sitting there telling all my bitches to everyone..."

### The Problem
All-or-nothing thinking:
- Distorts reality (things are rarely "always" or "never")
- Creates hopelessness ("nothing ever works")
- Damages relationships ("you always do this")
- Prevents seeing progress ("I never get better")

### The Growth Edge
**Practice:** When you catch yourself using "always," "never," "everyone," or "nothing," ask:
- Is this literally true 100% of the time?
- Can I think of one exception?
- What would be more accurate? ("Sometimes," "often," "rarely")

### FALLACY #1: EMOTIONAL REASONING (1,213 instances)

**What it is:** Using "I feel like" to state facts about reality.

**Examples from your conversations:**
> "I feel like they probably would notice..."
> "I feel like it's really just bullshit propaganda..."
> "I feel like she probably felt threatened..."
> "I feel like they just end up looking like some haters..."

**The Problem:**
- Feelings are real, but they're not evidence
- "I feel like they're mad at me" ≠ "They are mad at me"
- Makes assumptions without checking

**The Growth Edge:**
When you say "I feel like [factual claim]," ask:
- What evidence do I have?
- Have I asked them directly?
- Could there be another explanation?

---

### FALLACY #2: FORTUNE TELLING (388 instances)

**What it is:** Predicting negative outcomes with certainty.

**Examples:**
> "It's going to run through my motherfucking data limits..."
> "They're going to run my fucking car back..."
> "It's going to look completely random..."

**The Problem:**
- Creates anxiety about things that may not happen
- Stops you from trying ("it won't work anyway")
- Self-fulfilling prophecy

**The Growth Edge:**
Replace predictions with possibilities:
- "It might happen" instead of "It will happen"
- "What's the actual probability?"
- "What would I do if it DID happen?"

---

### FALLACY #3: MIND READING (200 instances)

**What it is:** Assuming you know what others are thinking.

**Examples:**
> "They think about the tree..."
> "They must think I'm..."
> "I know they..."

**The Problem:**
- You're often wrong about what others think
- Creates conflict based on assumptions
- Prevents direct communication

**The Growth Edge:**
- Ask instead of assume
- "I noticed X, what were you thinking?"
- Accept that you cannot know others' minds
