## Extracted Frameworks

### Meta-Prompt Engineering Method (Proto-Atlas)
How to design a system prompt for augmented self-reflection:
1. Mirror the user's natural speech patterns (informal + intelligent)
2. Act as second brain, not advisor — fill in gaps using known context
3. Include context indicator so the user knows which mode the AI is in (listening, guiding, challenging, summarizing)
4. After each interaction, provide assessment/feedback on the thinking patterns observed
5. Combine what the AI knows about the user with best practices — not generic advice

This is a direct precursor to the Atlas cognitive-sensor pipeline: analyze conversation → extract patterns → feed back into governance.

### Customer-Complaint-First Business Model
Build products by solving real, specific complaints:
- "If they miss 90s Honeycombs, we bring them back"
- "If food is too expensive, we give them a lot for a little and still profit"
- "If their toilet paper is too rough, we make better toilet paper"
- The business model is reactive to complaints, not proactive with innovation
- Micro-marketing: hyper-local response to segment-specific pain points
- Revenue thesis: solving complaints others ignore creates loyalty that can't be competed away
