## Extracted Frameworks

### Chain-of-Communication Warfare
Using indirect communication channels as a strategic tool:
- When someone can't reach you directly (blocked), they must go through intermediaries
- Each link in the chain adds delay, distortion, and dependency
- The person at the end of the chain (you) controls the pace — "she's gonna have to sit with it for nine days"
- The chain makes the aggressor look desperate/goofy to the intermediaries
- Key insight: the longer the chain, the more power you hold, because every intermediary sees the other person's escalation firsthand

### Proportional Boundary Enforcement
Boundaries aren't binary (on/off) — they're proportional to the violation history:
- "I forgave y'all for too much shit that I don't even hold over your head" — the grace period has a limit
- When boundaries finally come, they're experienced as extreme by the other person, but they're actually proportional to accumulated violations
- "I made it too easy for you because all you do is apologize" — accepting apologies without consequences trained the other person to keep violating
- Strategy: stop accepting apologies, start enforcing consequences, let the chain do the work

### Employee-First Business Model (Couchsurf Concept)
Early business ideation with a distinctive thesis:
- Internal finance/accounting department that educates employees on personal finance
- Company products distributed to employees (bundled toilet paper, water, vacuums)
- Employee-edition products at discounted prices through internal store
- Disruption angle: employees who use and believe in the products become organic advocates
- Self-sustaining model: employee purchases create a consistent internal revenue stream regardless of external market conditions
