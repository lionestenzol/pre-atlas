### 3. Door-to-Door Sales Mastery (Rich Material)

**The technique broken down:**

1. **Framing as technician, not salesperson** — bypassed the "I don't talk to salespeople" defense entirely. Showed up as someone providing a service, not selling one.

2. **Questions as setup** — "Do you have your phone on you? Grab it real quick, we'll run a speed test, only takes two seconds." The question IS the engagement. Each question leads to the next point. "The question is the answer."

3. **Smooth flow** — customers didn't realize they were being signed up until they were. "Oh, were you signing me up?" "Yes." And they were fine with it because the conversation felt natural.

4. **Emotion channeling** — good days, bad days, happy days, sad days — found a channel from ANY emotion directly to results. Competitive drive worked regardless of mood state.

5. **The fishbowl effect** — neighbors watched you talk to people. You controlled perception: even after a rejection, you walked away looking like you just made a sale. "I could have kept being told no, but they don't know that."

6. **Anti-thirst positioning** — "Oh yeah, I'm not tripping because I already hit my goal for the day." Not seeming desperate for the sale made customers trust more. Already having enough = not needing them = them wanting you.

7. **Using customers as social proof** — customers would hear coworkers calling you for help, see you as a leader. "It just makes me look more competent."

8. **Warm-up people** — used random interactions with people you weren't selling to as warm-ups for the real targets.

9. **Holding people there without aggression** — "If they don't want to talk to me, they're going to have to say it with their chest." Tone-deaf to soft rejection on purpose. Not aggressive, just present.

10. **Competitive framing with customers** — brought customers into the competition with coworkers. They wanted to help you win.

11. **Post-sale relationship** — called customers to check on service, asked if they knew anyone else who needed help. Framed referrals as "helping" not "selling."

12. **Gift psychology** — after the credit check, offered a gift and let them choose. Small gesture that cemented the relationship.

**Book themes:**
- Selling as a power dynamic — the person who controls the frame controls the outcome
- Confidence as the meta-skill — everything else flows from channeling the feeling of success
- Strategic perception management (fishbowl effect)
- Making the other person feel they chose, even when you led them there
