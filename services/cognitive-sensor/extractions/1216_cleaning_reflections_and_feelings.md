## Extracted Frameworks

### Unsustainable Standards Avoidance
Why high-performers sometimes avoid basic tasks:
- When someone models a task as unnecessarily hard (mother's cleaning), the task becomes associated with suffering
- The avoidance isn't laziness — it's a rejection of the modeled approach, not the task itself
- The fix: find your own efficient method. The user works smart AND hard, just not the "hard way for the sake of hard"
- Pattern: appears in cleaning, work, relationships — anywhere someone else's unsustainable standard was imposed

### Narcissist-as-Ungrown-Child
Reframing narcissistic behavior as developmental failure, not strategic evil:
- "Adults think oh this big scary person, but no, it's this big ass ungrown baby"
- Lying about dumb things, making everything about themselves, insecurity-driven sabotage — all childlike behaviors in an adult body
- The reframe reduces the narcissist's power: they're not sophisticated villains, they're toddlers with adult privileges
- Application: Destiny making the air fryer about herself = child wanting attention, not a calculated move

### Jealousy-Driven Withholding
When someone withholds something (cooking, help, praise) not because they can't provide it but because they feel inferior:
- Destiny didn't cook for him because she feared his cooking was better
- The withholding is preemptive self-protection: "if I don't try, I can't be compared"
- "It was never about the air fryers" — the surface conflict always masks the real insecurity
