## Extracted Frameworks

### Projection Reversal
When the dust settles on any relationship (work, friendship, romantic):
- You discover what you were projecting onto them vs. what they were projecting onto you
- High performers project capability and goodness onto others; low performers project insecurity and limitation
- "The shoes are on the other feet" — the mismatch becomes visible only after distance
- Key insight: the greatness you see in someone else is usually your own, reflected

### Outgrowth Detection Signals
How to know you've outgrown a relationship:
- You start pulling back naturally (less advice, fewer hangouts, saying no to invites)
- They notice the pullback but won't name it directly
- They claim mutual growth ("maybe we should grow apart") when only one person grew
- Secrecy increases — they stop sharing next moves because they're competing, not collaborating
- "I stopped being an open book" — the moment you stop volunteering information is the moment the relationship shifted

### Output-Based Identity
Identity built on measurable dominance:
- "I came, I saw, I conquered" — not aspirational, documented (3x output per member)
- The numbers aren't bragging — they're proof that the perceived weakness was always wrong
- "I just don't give a fuck" isn't apathy — it's the confidence that comes from knowing your output speaks louder than your personality
