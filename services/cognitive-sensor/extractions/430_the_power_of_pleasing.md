## Extracted Frameworks

### Reverse People-Pleasing
Using the language and posture of accommodation as a dominance tool:
- "Sorry I'm quiet" — said after deliberately starving someone of attention as punishment for disrespect
- "Sorry I couldn't help you" — said after intentionally withholding help from someone who was rude
- The apology is the weapon — it's polite enough that it can't be called out, but both parties know what it means
- Key insight: people-pleasing behavior is only submissive if the intent is genuine. When deployed strategically, it's one of the most disorienting power moves because the target can't articulate what just happened

### Energy Withdrawal Protocol
A step-by-step escalation response:
1. Start the interaction with full energy (talkative, engaged, warm)
2. When disrespect begins, make one subtle correction (saying "no" and continuing)
3. If disrespect continues, begin withdrawing energy (shorter responses, less eye contact, attention drifting)
4. As they escalate to fill the void, get progressively quieter
5. At peak escalation, deliver a calm question: "who are you talking to?" — the mirror
6. Close with politeness: "sorry I couldn't help you" — the coup de grâce
- The other person exhausts themselves escalating against nothing. They end the interaction drained; you end it having spent almost no energy.

### Controlling the Terms of Engagement
You don't have to fight on someone else's schedule:
- "I said tomorrow" — and everyone listened, including the aggressor and the crowd
- Refusing to fight in ROTC uniform = valuing what matters to you over responding to provocation
- The person who chooses when and where the conflict happens has already won the framing
- Variant: at Domino's and other jobs, "when they piss me off, I just work my ass off" = redirecting the aggression into output instead of reacting
- The provocation becomes fuel, but for YOUR priorities, not their entertainment

### Perceived Weakness as Bait (Passive Trap)
Recurring across every environment:
- People interpret quietness, politeness, or being new as weakness
- They test, provoke, escalate based on that read
- The user lets them build confidence in their wrong assessment
- When the correction comes, it's devastating because they overcommitted to a false model
- "He was getting off on thinking he could keep talking shit" — the provocation felt like winning to the aggressor, until it wasn't
- This is the same pattern from 546 (Anger) and 1075 (Psychology) seen from the pleasing angle
