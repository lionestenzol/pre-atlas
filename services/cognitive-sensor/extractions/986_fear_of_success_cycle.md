## Extracted Frameworks

### Fear of Success > Fear of Failure
The real blocker for high-capability people isn't inability — it's anticipating the consequences of winning:
- Success changes relationships (people who were comfortable with you struggling won't be comfortable with you succeeding)
- Success creates new responsibilities and visibility
- "I know I can get what I want" removes the excuse of inability — what remains is the weight of actually having it
- The procrastination isn't laziness — it's managing the transition cost of the next level

### Escape Hatch Rejection
Pattern of refusing obvious exit ramps:
- Job opportunities that would solve immediate problems, turned down because "I wasn't done yet"
- The "not done yet" isn't stubbornness — it's recognizing that leaving early means the lesson is incomplete
- Staying in bad situations past the obvious exit point is data collection, not masochism
- The pattern only looks irrational from outside; from inside, there's information still being gathered

### Contingency Plan Architecture
Strategy as a decision tree, not a linear plan:
- "A series of contingency plans all aimed upward" — every branch leads up, just through different paths
- No single point of failure because no single path to success
- This is the same architecture as Atlas governance: multiple routes to the same mode transition
- Allows for the unpredictability thesis (from 969) — you don't need to know which path you'll take because they all go up

### AI Project Management = Atlas Origin Wish
The literal request that Atlas fulfills:
- "Combine everything into one" = federated monorepo (cognitive-sensor + delta-kernel + executor)
- "AI assistant for project management" = governance daemon + CycleBoard + daily routing
- "Solopreneur" = autonomous execution without a team, just systems
- This conversation is the **requirements document** for Atlas, expressed as a wish

### Dominance-Attraction Paradox
People attempt to dominate you BECAUSE of attraction, not despite it:
- "Very dominant even though very unassuming" — the combination triggers both desire and threat
- Domination attempts are bids for control over something they find compelling
- The paradox: the more unassuming you are, the more dominant you actually appear (because the restraint implies capability)
- Connects to quiet-as-weapon (546) and perceived-weakness-as-bait (430)
